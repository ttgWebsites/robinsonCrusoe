Themes

  • The importance of maintaining self-awareness
  • When Crusoe is stranded on the island he does not revert his way of life to that of a brute as one would think a person left alone with no modern supplies would
  • Instead Crusoe manages to maintain a suitable living by keeping aware of himself and his humanity
  • Crusoe's ability to remain being self aware is ultimately what allows him to survive and keep his sanity
  • He manages to build shelter, farm, and domesticate animals
  • The importance of repenting
  • Crusoe believes he has made many mistakes in his life, the main one being not listening to his father and becoming a lawyer
  • He believes he is being punished for this by being sent to the island, and that the reason he has not died is that he has not atoned for his sins
  • Crusoe believes he must survive the island, alter his life, and constantly seek Gods forgiveness
  • Crusoe's survival and triumphant return home helps repent for his past sins
  • The idea of mastery
  • The reader sees the idea of mastery in both a positive and negative light
  • When Crusoe is subjected to living on the island he must master his skills in many different aspects in order to survive
  • He teaches himself to farm with nothing, build suitable shelter (even builds a basement) and manages to domesticate animals as well
  • We also see mastery as a negative when Crusoe exercises control over the slave Friday
  • Though Crusoe and Friday get along fine, Crusoe shows his dominance and superiority
  • Mastery is important to the book whether good or bad
