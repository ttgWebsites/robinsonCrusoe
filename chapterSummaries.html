Chapter Summaries

  • Chapter 1
  • Robinson Crusoe begins his story at his birth in 1632 in York, England
  • He is the youngest of three brothers and as a result would inherit very little
  • His father suggests he study law but Crusoe is set on going to see, to both of his parents disdain
  • He first decides he will follow his fathers will, but is overcome with temptation and goes to see in 1651, however he nearly dies in a storm, and after surviving he believes its fate that he give up sea travel
  • Chapter 2
  • However after traveling to London, Crusoe is tempted into going back to sea on a merchant voyage, he takes 40 pounds worth of items to sell as an investment from his parents and in turn nets 300 pounds
  • He keeps 100 and leaves the remaining 200 pounds with a widow in London who he trusts before setting off on another expedition
  • Moorish pirates near Sallee in Africa overtake Crusoe’s ship and enslave him as a fisherman
  • One day after the ship gets lost in fog, a compass is put on board, as well as gun powder, Crusoe waits for an opportunity to escape
  • Chapter 3
  • Crusoe sets off on a fishing trip with two other slaves, Ismael and Xury
  • Crusoe pushes Ismael in the water and threatens to shoot him if he doesn't swim to shore
  • He then turns to Xury and asks for his loyalty, the two sale south along the shore, and at one point spot wild animals
  • Crusoe kills a lion and takes its skin, they then continue on until they come across possibly Cape Verde or the Canary Islands where they see naked black people on shore
  • They are given food by the people, and Crusoe kills a leopard which impresses the natives who in turn skin the leopard for Crusoe
  • He recognizes a European ship off the coast, and him and Xury are picked up by a Portuguese Captain
  • The Captain takes Crusoe to Brazil, and buys Xury and the animal pelts for a large sum of money
  • Chapter 4
  • After a 22 day voyage Crusoe arrives in Brazil with many gifts from The Captain
  • Crusoe befriends a white neighbor, and decides he will become a tobacco farmer
  • He has a meager living his first two years yet prospers his third, he does however regret selling Xury who could have made life easier
  • The Captain, remembering the 200 pounds Crusoe has in London, has 100 sent to Crusoe as well as many gifts
  • Crusoe is well off after the help from The Captain and decides to go on a journey to Guinea to acquire slaves for his plantation
  • Chapter 5
  • Crusoe writes a will leaving half of his possessions to The Captain before leaving for Guinea in 1659
  • He brings many trinkets with which to buy slaves however the journey is short lived
  • The ship encounters two storms the second destroying the ship and putting the men in life boats to try and reach shore
  • Crusoe is the only survivor and washes up on an island where he is alone
  • Chapter 6
  • After resting on the island Crusoe returns to the beached ship and looks for supplies, he builds a raft with wood from the ship and rafts all the supplies he can to a cove
  • He discovers he is completely alone on the island except for some animals, and soon after collecting all he can from the ship, it's remains are washed away
  • Chapter 7
  • Crusoe worried about savages, and needing a place to stay build a “fortress” safe from animals and with visibility of the sea
  • His shelter is rather elaborate and he stores all his supplies there and even builds a cellar
  • Crusoe discovers goats on the island which he kills for food
  • Crusoe makes a cross marking the date of his arrival, September 30, 1659, and he also keeps a journal of his experience
  • He watches for ships but is always disappointed
  • Chapter 8
  • Crusoe invited the reader into his journal and narrates some events that had previously been narrated
  • He tells of losing track of the Sabbath, the building of furniture and tools, and the taming of a goat
  • Chapter 9
  • Crusoe continues his journal, he describes his attempts to tame a pigeon, manufacture candles, and the discovery of barley on the island
  • He uses the barley to make bread which he uses for sustenance
  • On April 16th an earthquake shakes the island nearly killing Crusoe, however he survives and his possessions are safe
  • Chapter 10
  • Following the earthquake a hurricane hits the island, however Crusoe manages to survive that also as
  • He is worried that another earthquake may destroy his home so resolves to move it, however he discovers that the wreckage of his ship has washed back ashore so he salvages for more items rather than move his house
  • Chapter 11
  • Crusoe becomes very ill after a long spell of rain, he is nearly to the point of death, and begins to hallucinate of a man descending from a black cloud on a flame
  • The man in his vision tells Crusoe that he has been saved from death because he has yet to receive repentance from his suffering
  • He finally says a real prayer to God asking for his salvation, and the next day finally begins to recover
  • After some deep thought, bible reading, and prayer he finally manages a recovery and realizes that his isolation on the island is a deliverance from his sinful life
  • Chapter 12
  • Now in July of 1660 Crusoe decides to take full advantage of his exile and explore the island
  • He searches and finds fruit which he collects, he also discovers a valley where he thinks about building another shelter
  • He now is surrounded by cats as well
  • It is finally a whole year since he landed on the island and he fasts for a whole day to honor that
  • Crusoe eventually abandons his diary after he runs out of ink
  • Chapter 13
  • Crusoe comes up with a calendar of the dry and rainy months in order to plan his crops better
  • He also realizes that stakes he put in the ground years ago have aided the growth of hedges and shade
  • Crusoe then teaches himself to make wicker baskets, the only supplies he lacks are pipes, glassware, and a kettle
  • Chapter 14
  • Crusoe finally sets out to survey the island, he discovers he can make out land off in the distance which he believes to be part of Spanish America, yet he doesn't make plans to explore that out of fear of cannibals
  • Crusoe now has lived on the island for nearly two years and has had alternating moments of despair and satisfaction
  • He still reads the bible and has faith in God
  • Chapter 15
  • Continuing to build things, Crusoe spends a few months building shelving, at this time though he is having problems with birds harming his crops so he kills a few birds and keeps them out to scare away other birds
  • He finally teaches himself how to make bread with the supplies he has grown, and also teaches himself basic pottery
  • Crusoe finally decides he should go to the mainland but can not turn the wrecked ship right side up so his efforts are futile
  • Chapter 16
  • The decision is made to build a canoe so Crusoe spends month chopping branches and shaping wood, he builds a giant canoe yet can not move it and must build a smaller one
  • It has now been 4 years since he reached the island and Crusoe begins to reflect on how blessed he has been and how far he has come even though he is alone
  • He decides to make himself new clothes and an umbrella, and after finishing his new canoe decides take a tour of the island
  • He is nearly washed to sea yet manages to make it back to the island where he is grateful, and greeted by his parrot
  • Chapter 17
  • Afraid of being lost at sea Crusoe spends the next year content on the island, he decides in order to survive he must tame goats as he will eventually run out of bullets to kill them
  • He manages to grow a flock, and use them for dairy products as well as food, and this control over the goats gives him a feeling of great power
  • Crusoe gives a list of all his island possessions, his two plantations, a grape arbor, crops and a goat pen
  • Chapter 18
  • One day Crusoe comes across a foot print in the sand which terrifies him as he believes it may be the devil, but even the thought of a human terrifies him
  • He becomes very paranoid and builds fortification on his dwellings as well as a tunnel to keep his goats over night
  • He lives in fear for two whole years
  • Chapter 19
  • Crusoe comes across a spread of human remains on a beach and realizes that there might be cannibals around
  • He plans to keep watch and shoot them down, but then realizes he is in fact in no danger, and has no right to judge, and doesn't want to start an invasion by savages
  • Chapter 20
  • Crusoe makes all of his efforts to keep the cannibals from finding him, he hides, removes traces of activities, and even cooks under ground
  • He finds a cave that he likes very much and he thinks it keeps him hidden, however one day he is shocked to see a pair of eyes staring at him
  • He is extremely scared, yet it turns out to be a goat
  • Crusoe later returns to the beach and sees 9 savages feasting on human remains, he goes to confront them but they leave out to sea
  • Chapter 21
  • One afternoon Crusoe is reading from the bible when he hears 2 gun shots
  • He believes it to be a ship so he builds a fire, but he later determines the ships men are either gone or dead, so he canoes out to the ship and searches it
  • The ship has large quantities of wine, clothing, and treasures, which he drags back to his home
  • Chapter 22
  • Crusoe has a dream that a victim is hunted by 11 cannibals on his island, and that the victim comes to Crusoe for help
  • About a year and a half later something very similar happens, 30 cannibals are on the island ready to slaughter 2 victims, however one escapes and runs towards Crusoe's dwelling
  • Crusoe kills two cannibals who chase the man, and buries them
  • He then has the man he saves pledge his devotion to him
  • Chapter 23
  • Crusoe names the man Friday and takes care of him teaching him simple English and feeding him
  • Crusoe is glad to have a companion, and he makes sure to convey to Friday the moral issues of cannibalism
  • Friday is also helpful to Crusoe's farming
  • Chapter 24
  • Crusoe and Friday grow close, there relationship also allows Crusoe to learn some about the cannibal's patterns, and they travel to the island often.
  • Crusoe also discovers they are somewhere near Trinidad
  • He teaches Friday about God and realizes that Friday has learned similar beliefs, although he doesn't know much about the devil or evil
  • Crusoe learns that some men from his ship wreck survived
  • The two men, Crusoe and Friday, fear losing one another, so they agree to travel together to the native land at the end of the year
  • Chapter 25
  • Shortly before Friday and Crusoe plan to journey to the natives land the Cannibals return to take 3 prisoners from the island
  • Crusoe finally decides he must do something, so he and Friday overpower the cannibals with superior weapons, taking all but 4 of them
  • They then rescue the prisoners discovering that one is Friday's father and the other a Spaniard
  • Crusoe feeds them and takes care of them, and they all become loyal subjects to Crusoe
  • Chapter 26
  • Crusoe discusses with the new men a plan to return to Europe, they decide that they will also try to save the captured men on the cannibals land
  • They realize that to leave with that many men they must increase there food supply so they begin growing more food
  • Chapter 27
  • Later on a boat is seen approaching the island, and it appears to be English
  • Crusoe investigates after realizing three men are held captive on the boat
  • It turns out it is the captain and 2 crew members who have been mutinied
  • Crusoe saves them in hopes they will take Crusoe and his men to England
  • Crusoe devises a plan to round up all the mutineers and take over there boat
  • His plan works and he and his men eventually surround them and force them to surrender, offering to spare all there lives except the ring leader
  • Chapter 28
  • Crusoe with the help of his men, the prisoners he saved, and the captured mutineers takes over the English ship
  • Crusoe is overwhelmed by the ship, and he now has the opportunity to sail home
  • He and his group of men set sail for England and leave the mutineers on the island so as they wont be killed for mutiny in England
  • They leave on December 19, 1686, 28 years after he first set foot on the island
  • Back in England Crusoe finds the widow who is still keeping his money, he also finds out his family is dead other than two sisters and his brothers children
  • He heads to Libson to find out what happened to his Brazilian plantations
  • Chapter 29
  • Crusoe reaches Libson and finds the Portuguese Captain who he had made a benefactor
  • Crusoe learns that his land has been placed in trust and in turn have made lots of money
  • The captain owes Crusoe money so he pays him back, however Crusoe is moved by the honesty of the Captain's actions that he gives him back some of the money
  • Crusoe manages to reclaim all his wealth from Brazil, he sends some back to his family and the widow in England, and then he decides to move back to England but he decides to travel by land, fearful of sea travel
  • Chapter 30
  • Crusoe and his men leave Libson and travel north towards England
  • They encounter harsh weather, and dangerous creatures, and at one point are surrounded by 300 wolves
  • They manage to fight them back with guns and explosions, which is rather miraculous, and the finally reach Toulouse, France
  • Chapter 31
  • Crusoe reaches England and sells his Brazilian plantation for a huge sum, yet he is restless for the life of traveling
  • He settles down for a while, even marrying, but after his wife dies, he decides to head to the East Indies to trade
  • On his way there he stops at his island and discovers the Spaniards have taken great care of it, they have fended off cannibals and are prospering
