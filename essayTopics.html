Essay Topics

  • Though Crusoe spends nearly all his time on the island trying to leave, when he finally is able to his return home is anticlimactic and it seems he is only interested in returning to the island. How does his attitude about the island change?
  • When Crusoe first lands on the island he is alone and must struggle to survive at first.
  • However after spending many years surviving and building a home on the island Crusoe seems to become accustomed to living alone and being self sufficient.
  • Crusoe seems to forget what life is like away from the island as he begins to cherish being alone.
  • When he sees the first sign of other human life on the island he is fearful and panics.
  • He begins to views other humans as threats as he comes to enjoy his life of power and solitude.
  • Therefore when he finally is able to leave the island he only longs of returning so he can be alone again.
  • Throughout the novel it seems as if Crusoe is uninterested in aesthetics and has no appreciation for beauty, what effect does this have on the novel?
  • Crusoe seems to lack any interest in any of the beauty his island home seems to offer.
  • He spends very little time describing it, and when he does it is more so matter of fact.
  • Crusoe even seems to lack any interest in beauty in an abstract form such as morality and heroism.
  • The text seems to show that Defoe may as well lack the eye for beauty just like his protagonist Crusoe.
  • This indifference is quite a difference from traditional literature for that time period and goes to show that art has many more purposes than just showing the beauty in life.
  • Instead describing everyday life can also prove captivating and entertaining
