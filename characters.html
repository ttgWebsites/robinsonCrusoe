Characters

  • Robinson Crusoe
  • The narrator and protagonist of the novel
  • Middle class young man growing up in New York trying to figure out his life, he's urged to be in law by his father yet has dreams of a life as a merchant at sea
  • He has to fight himself and his guilt for not obeying his fathers will
  • A very steady man when it comes to doing things and his work is done in a plodding sort of way
  • He has a lot of perseverance which helps him survive the stories adventures
  • Friday
  • A young Caribbean man who is also cannibalistic
  • Crusoe converts him to Protestantism
  • Becomes a servant for Crusoe after his life is saved from being eaten by other cannibals
  • He does not resent being Crusoe's servant after being saved, and he has a charisma that contrasts with Crusoe's rather bland character
  • The Portuguese Captain
  • Sea captain who rescues Crusoe and Xury after they escape from captors
  • He takes Crusoe to Brazil where Crusoe becomes a plantation owner
  • Polite, and very personable, but somewhat lacking of charisma
  • A good man, he helps Crusoe by paying him far to much for a slave and animal skins, as well as helping take care of his Brazilian investments
  • The Spaniard
  • A crewman on a Spanish ship that is wrecked off Crusoe's Island
  • He is originally destined to be eaten by the Cannibals who rescue him, however Crusoe saves him by making him a “subject” in his “kingdom”
  • Xury
  • He is a slave boy who is briefly enslaved with Crusoe in Sallee
  • Crusoe seems to developed a trust for him, however when they are both rescued by The Captain, Crusoe sells Xury for a rather large sum
  • The Widow
  • She appears at two points in the novel
  • Crusoe leaves 200 pounds with her in England, and she keeps watch, returning it to him after his 35 year absence
